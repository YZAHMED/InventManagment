üìù My Design Notes:

  • Users Table: Stores all user accounts who can access the system
  • Items Table: Keeps track of inventory items and their quantities
  • Relationship: I decided on a simple design where users can manage items (though I didn't implement user-specific items yet - maybe for future enhancement?)
  • Security: Passwords are hashed, not stored as plain text!

üîí Database Constraints & Rules

Primary Keys:

  • USERS.Id - Auto-increment, unique identifier
  • ITEMS.Id - Auto-increment, unique identifier

Unique Constraints:

  • Username must be unique (no duplicate usernames)
  • Email must be unique (one account per email)

Data Validation:

  • Quantity cannot be negative
  • Username and Email are required fields
  • ImagePath is optional (can be NULL)

üöÄ Future Improvements I'm Thinking About:

  • Add a Categories table to organize items better

  • Add a Transactions table to track who changed what
  • Maybe add Suppliers table to track where items come from

üí° What I Learned:

This was my first time designing a database from scratch! I tried to keep it simple but functional. I made sure to include proper primary keys and thought about data integrity with unique constraints. The hardest part was deciding on the data types - I wasn't sure if I should use VARCHAR with specific lengths or just use MAX for simplicity. I went with a mix approach based on what made sense for each field.

I also realized that normalization is pretty important - I could have put everything in one big table but separating users and items makes way more sense for maintenance and scalability.
